Research Projects

Our lab investigates the triggers and immunological mechanisms behind allergic reactions using advanced molecular techniques. Here are a few of our current projects:

Basophil and mast cell biology

We dissect the molecular and functional programs that control mast cell and basophil activation in allergic disease. Our work integrates primary human cells, mouse models, and clinical studies to define mechanisms driving anaphylaxis.

Biomarkers of immediate hypersensitivity reactions

We aim to discover sensitive biomarkers for acute allergic reactions, including anaphylaxis, to improve diagnosis and clinical management.

Anaphylaxis prevention and treatment

Our lab investigates targeting the IgE–FcεRI signaling pathway to prevent and rescue anaphylaxis. BTK inhibition has shown complete suppression of mast cell and basophil activation in both preclinical and clinical studies.
